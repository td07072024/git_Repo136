The list follows McKenna and Bell's Classification of Mammals for prehistoric genera (1997)[16] and Wozencraft (2005) in Wilson and Reeders Mammal Species of the World for extant genera.[17] The percrocutids are, in contrast to McKenna and Bell's classification, not included as a subfamily into the Hyaenidae, but as the separate family Percrocutidae (though they are generally grouped as sister-taxa to hyenas[18]). Furthermore, the living brown hyena and its closest extinct relatives are not included in the genus Pachycrocuta, but in the genus Parahyaena. However, some research has suggested Parahyaena may be synonymous with Pachycrocuta, making the brown hyena the only extant member of this genus.[19]
